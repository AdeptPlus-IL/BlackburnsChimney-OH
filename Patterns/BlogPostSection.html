The Three Levels of Chimney Inspection

Not all chimney inspections are created equal. The Chimney Safety Institute of America (CSIA) defines three levels of inspection, each suited to different circumstances and covering different scopes of examination. Understanding which level applies to your situation is the first step to making sure your chimney gets the attention it actually needs.

Level 1 — The Annual Inspection

A Level 1 inspection is the standard annual checkup for a chimney in regular use with no changes to the appliance or fuel type. The technician examines readily accessible portions of the chimney interior and exterior — looking at the firebox, damper, smoke chamber, and visible flue lining for obvious deterioration, blockages, or unsafe conditions. No special equipment is required.

For most Columbus homeowners who use their fireplace regularly and have it serviced annually, a Level 1 inspection is the right choice. If you haven't had the chimney inspected in several years, or recently purchased the home, you may need a more thorough assessment.

Level 2 — The Comprehensive Inspection

A Level 2 inspection covers everything in a Level 1, plus a camera-assisted examination of the full flue from the firebox to the chimney cap. The video camera allows the technician to see the entire interior of the flue lining — including physically inaccessible areas — and identify cracks, spalling, gaps, or deterioration that a visual check would miss entirely.

A Level 2 inspection is required in four specific situations:

  • When buying or selling a home with a chimney or fireplace
  • After any chimney fire, regardless of apparent severity
  • After a natural event such as an earthquake or lightning strike
  • When changing the fuel type or replacing the appliance connected to the chimney

In the Columbus real estate market, Level 2 inspections have become standard. Many buyers request them as a condition of purchase, and sellers with a recent report on file can address issues proactively — avoiding last-minute repair negotiations at closing.

Level 3 — The Invasive Inspection

A Level 3 inspection is reserved for situations where Levels 1 and 2 have identified a hazard that cannot be fully assessed without removing portions of the chimney structure. This level is uncommon, typically follows a chimney fire or structural damage, and involves partial demolition. It is substantially more disruptive and expensive than Levels 1 or 2.

What Happens During a Level 2 Inspection?

A Level 2 inspection with Blackburns typically takes one to two hours. Here's what to expect:

  1. 1.
    Exterior examination. The technician begins outside, assessing the chimney crown, cap, flashing, mortar joints, and visible masonry for signs of deterioration or damage.
  2. 2.
    Interior firebox and smoke chamber. The accessible interior components are examined — damper operation, firebox condition, and smoke shelf for creosote buildup and structural integrity.
  3. 3.
    Camera-assisted flue inspection. A video camera is inserted into the flue and advanced the full length of the liner. The technician reviews footage in real time, noting cracks, gaps, spalling, or obstructions.
  4. 4.
    Documentation and report. After the inspection you receive a plain-language written report with photos or video stills documenting each component's condition. If issues are found, the report explains what was found, where it is, and what the recommended course of action is.

When Should You Schedule One?

If any of the following apply, a Level 2 inspection is the appropriate next step:

  • You are buying or selling a home with a chimney
  • You've had a chimney fire — even a small one
  • You haven't had an inspection in more than two years
  • You've recently moved into the home and don't know its service history
  • You've switched to a different fuel or replaced the insert or stove

When in doubt, start with a Level 1. If the technician identifies concerns, they'll recommend whether a Level 2 is warranted. Blackburns' technicians never upsell — if a Level 1 gives us a clear picture, that's what we'll tell you.
