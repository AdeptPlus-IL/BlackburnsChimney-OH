OUR PROCESS

Our Inspection Process

Every chimney inspection at Blackburns follows a rigorous, standardized process developed by CSIA and adapted to your home's specific needs.

Initial Assessment

We evaluate your fireplace type, usage frequency, and any concerns you've noticed—like drafting issues, odors, or visible damage.

Camera-Assisted Inspection

Using specialized video equipment, we examine the entire flue, firebox, and exterior chimney structure, recording footage so you can see exactly what we see.

Detailed Analysis

Our technicians assess creosote buildup, structural integrity, cracks, leaks, blockages, and airflow to identify safety hazards and performance issues.

Clear Reporting

We provide a comprehensive written report with photos and plain-language explanations of findings and recommendations for repairs or maintenance.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions About Chimney Inspections

How often should I have my chimney inspected this time?
The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends a chimney inspection at least once a year if you use your fireplace regularly. 
If you use it occasionally, annual inspection is still recommended as a safety best practice. We can advise on frequency based on your specific usage.

What's the difference between a Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 inspection?
Level 1 is a basic visual inspection of readily accessible areas. Level 2 includes interior camera inspection, exterior examination, and evaluation of connections—this is often required for real estate transactions. Level 3 is an in-depth inspection with removal of components or structural access when a Level 2 reveals concerns. We perform all three types based on your needs.
Will the inspection damage my fireplace or home?
No. Our inspection process is non-invasive and non-destructive. We use protective runners and take great care to keep your home clean. Our technicians are trained to work respectfully in your space.
How long does an inspection take?
Most inspections take 45 minutes to 1.5 hours, depending on chimney complexity, accessibility, and whether we identify issues that require further evaluation. We'll give you a timeframe when you schedule.
What if the inspection finds problems?
We provide a detailed report with photos and written recommendations. We can discuss repair options, timelines, and costs so you can make informed decisions. Many issues can be addressed promptly to avoid safety risks.
