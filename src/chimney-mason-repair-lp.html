Structural Integrity

What Is the Freeze-Thaw Cycle?

Your brickwork is naturally porous, soaking in water throughout the year. Once temperatures drop below freezing, that trapped water expands, exerting extra pressure on the surrounding brick and mortar. When temperatures rise again, the ice melts and the material contracts. This repeated expansion and contraction (sometimes occurring multiple times in a single day) gradually widens cracks, crumbles mortar joints, and causes brick faces to spall and break away.
