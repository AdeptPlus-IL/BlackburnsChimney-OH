THE PROBLEM

Do Fireplace Inserts Boost Heating Efficiency?

Traditional fireplaces look beautiful but lose up to 90% of heat up the chimney. If your fireplace is outdated, rarely used, or drafty, you're heating the neighborhood instead of your home.

Signs your fireplace isn't working efficiently:

  • ◆Cold drafts coming from the fireplace even when it's not in use
  • ◆High heating bills despite regular use of your fireplace
  • ◆Uneven room temperatures—some areas stay cold
  • ◆Smoke or odors backing into the living space
  • ◆A chimney that pulls warm air out of your home in winter
  • ◆An outdated wood-burning or gas fireplace you rarely light

A modern fireplace insert solves these problems. Inserts fit into your existing fireplace opening, capturing and distributing heat efficiently throughout your home. Blackburns' technicians assess your current fireplace, recommend the right insert for your needs, and install it with precision to maximize performance and safety.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Fireplace Inserts — Frequently Asked Questions

What types of fireplace inserts do you install?

We install three primary types of fireplace inserts, each suited to different homes and lifestyles:

  • Wood-burning inserts – ideal for homeowners with a working chimney who love the authentic crackle, aroma, and radiant heat of a traditional fire. These deliver high heat output and work well during power outages.
  • Gas inserts – convenient, clean-burning, and easy to operate with a switch or remote. Available in natural gas or propane, with realistic ceramic logs and adjustable flame settings.
  • Pellet inserts – an eco-friendly option that burns compressed wood pellets with minimal ash and consistent heat output. Great for homeowners who want efficiency without splitting firewood.

During your in-home consultation, we evaluate your chimney, venting, fuel preferences, and heating goals before recommending the best fit.

Can a fireplace insert fit into my existing fireplace?

In most cases, yes – but a proper fit depends on several factors we evaluate during inspection:

  • Firebox dimensions – height, width, and depth must accommodate the insert with proper clearances.
  • Chimney condition – the flue, liner, and masonry must be safe and structurally sound, or repaired before installation.
  • Flue size and venting – most inserts require a stainless-steel liner sized to the appliance for safe draft and efficiency.
  • Hearth and surround – must meet code clearances to combustible materials.

If your chimney needs relining, tuckpointing, or a new cap before installation, we'll explain the work clearly and can complete everything in-house so you only deal with one trusted team.

How much will a fireplace insert save me on heating costs?

Most Columbus homeowners see meaningful savings after switching to a modern insert. Actual results depend on:

  • Your current setup – replacing an open masonry fireplace yields the biggest gains, since open fireplaces lose up to 90% of heat up the flue.
  • Fuel type and local energy prices – wood, gas, and pellet costs vary by season and supplier.
  • Usage patterns – homes that zone-heat with the insert during peak hours see the largest reductions.
  • Home size and insulation – a quality insert can effectively heat up to 2,000 sq ft.

Typically, regular winter use results in a 15–30% reduction in heating bills, with some households saving even more by reducing reliance on central heating.

How long does installation take?

Most fireplace insert installations are completed in a single day. A typical project includes:

  • Pre-installation chimney inspection to confirm the system is safe and ready.
  • Any necessary repairs or relining – including stainless-steel liner installation sized to the insert.
  • Insert placement and connection with proper clearances and venting hookups.
  • Gas line connection (for gas inserts) by a certified technician.
  • Final testing and a walkthrough so you know exactly how to operate and maintain your new insert.

Larger projects involving extensive masonry work or custom surrounds may take longer. We'll provide a clear timeline during your consultation so there are no surprises on installation day.

Do you offer warranties on fireplace inserts?

Yes – we back every installation with strong warranty coverage on both the product and our workmanship:

  • Manufacturer warranties – typically 5–10 years on major components, depending on the brand and model. Some manufacturers offer lifetime coverage on fireboxes.
  • Labor warranty – we stand behind every installation we perform, ensuring your insert vents, drafts, and operates correctly.
  • Ongoing support – if anything ever feels off, our CSIA-certified team is available to diagnose and resolve issues promptly.

We only sell premium inserts from trusted manufacturers, so warranty claims (when needed) are straightforward and reliable.
