THE PROBLEM

Damaged Masonry Doesn't Fix Itself

Chimney masonry takes a beating year after year — freeze-thaw cycles, moisture infiltration, and years of heat exposure wear down mortar joints, brick faces, and the chimney crown.

Signs your masonry needs attention:

  • ◆Crumbling or missing mortar between bricks (spalling)
  • ◆White staining (efflorescence) on the chimney exterior
  • ◆Cracked or deteriorating chimney crown
  • ◆Brick faces flaking or breaking apart
  • ◆Water stains on interior walls near the fireplace
  • ◆Visible gaps in the flashing at the roofline

The good news: most masonry damage is repairable when caught early. Blackburns' technicians assess the full extent of damage — from the crown to the base — and recommend only the work that's actually needed.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Masonry Services — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my chimney masonry needs repair?

Several visible warning signs suggest your chimney masonry needs attention. The most common include:

  • White staining (efflorescence) on the brick exterior – a clear indicator water is moving through the masonry
  • Crumbling, recessed, or missing mortar between bricks
  • Visible cracks in the chimney crown (the concrete cap at the top)
  • Flaking, pitting, or spalling brick faces where the outer layer is breaking apart
  • Water stains on interior walls or ceilings near your fireplace
  • Rust on the damper or firebox components
  • Gaps or lifted flashing where the chimney meets the roof

If you're noticing any of these, a professional inspection is the right first step. Many of these signs point to moisture infiltration – and the longer water gets in, the more expensive the eventual repair becomes.

What is tuckpointing and do I need it?

Tuckpointing (sometimes called repointing) is the process of carefully grinding out deteriorated mortar from the joints between bricks and replacing it with fresh, color-matched mortar. It's one of the most common and cost-effective chimney repairs – and one of the most important, because mortar joints are usually the first part of a chimney to fail.

You likely need tuckpointing if:

  • Mortar joints are recessed, crumbling, or missing in places
  • You can scrape mortar out with a screwdriver or your fingernail
  • Hairline cracks are running through the joints
  • The mortar has turned sandy or powdery to the touch

Done properly, tuckpointing restores the structural integrity of the chimney, blocks water entry, and can add decades of life – saving you from a much costlier full or partial rebuild later on.

Can you match the color of my existing brick and mortar?

Yes – color matching is part of every masonry repair we do. Our technicians carefully evaluate your existing brick and mortar to select replacement materials that blend as naturally as possible with the surrounding chimney.

A few things to know about matching:

  • Mortar color is achieved through a combination of cement, sand, and pigment selected to match your existing joints
  • Brick matching depends on age, manufacturer, and weathering – for older chimneys we can often source reclaimed brick when needed
  • Newly placed mortar will look slightly brighter for the first few months before it weathers in
  • A perfect, invisible match isn't always achievable on older chimneys, but our goal is always a repair that looks intentional and consistent

We'll walk you through expectations before any work begins so there are no surprises.

How long does a masonry repair take?

Most masonry repairs are completed faster than homeowners expect. General timelines look like this:

  • Partial tuckpointing: half a day to a full day, depending on access
  • Full tuckpointing of a chimney: 1–2 days
  • Crown sealing or resurfacing: half a day to a full day
  • Full crown rebuild: 1–2 days, plus cure time before use
  • Partial brick replacement: typically 1 day
  • Partial or full chimney rebuild: 2–5 days depending on size

Weather plays a significant role – masonry materials need temperatures above roughly 40°F and dry conditions to cure properly. We'll always give you a clear timeline up front and notify you immediately if weather requires a reschedule.

Is chimney masonry repair covered by homeowners insurance?

Coverage depends on your specific policy and the cause of the damage. As a general guide:

  • Often covered: sudden, accidental damage – lightning strikes, fallen trees, vehicle impact, severe storms, or chimney fires
  • Usually not covered: gradual wear and tear, age-related deterioration, freeze-thaw damage that built up over time, or damage tied to deferred maintenance
  • Sometimes covered: damage caused by a covered event that exposed pre-existing issues – these claims can go either way

To help with your claim, we provide:

  • Detailed written inspection reports
  • Photo and video documentation of the damage
  • Itemized repair estimates
  • Direct communication with your adjuster if needed

We always recommend calling your insurance carrier before scheduling repairs so you understand exactly what's covered.
