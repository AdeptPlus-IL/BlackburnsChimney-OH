COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions About Chimney Service in Pickerington

How often should I have my chimney inspected?
The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends annual inspections for chimneys in regular use. If you use your fireplace frequently, a yearly checkup ensures everything is safe and efficient. We also provide Level 2 inspections for real estate transactions.
What is creosote and why does it need to be removed?
Creosote is a flammable byproduct of burning wood that accumulates on the inside of your chimney. Heavy buildup is a leading cause of chimney fires, which can damage the structure and create serious safety hazards. Regular cleaning removes creosote and keeps your home protected.
What does a Level 2 chimney inspection include?
A Level 2 inspection is a more thorough examination than a basic Level 1 inspection. It includes video camera inspection of the flue, a detailed assessment of the chimney structure, checking for cracks or damage, and evaluating the damper and other components. Many real estate transactions require a Level 2 inspection.
Why is my fireplace smoking into the house?
Smoking is often caused by draft problems, a blocked flue, a damaged damper, or poor ventilation. It can also result from a chimney that's too short for the home or air pressure issues. Our diagnostic testing identifies the exact cause so we can recommend the right fix.
Can you help with real estate inspections?
Yes. We provide Level 2 chimney inspections required for many home sales and purchases. These detailed inspections give buyers confidence and help sellers prove their chimney is safe and well-maintained.
