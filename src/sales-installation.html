COMMON QUESTIONS

Fireplace & Stove Installation — Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a fireplace, insert, and heating stove?
A traditional fireplace is a masonry or pre-fabricated firebox built into the home. A fireplace insert fits inside an existing fireplace to improve efficiency and heat output. A heating stove is a standalone unit that can be placed in a room or against a wall. Each has different venting, clearance, and installation requirements—we help you choose the best option for your home.
How long does a fireplace or stove installation typically take?
Most installations are completed in a single visit—typically 4 to 8 hours depending on the unit type, existing venting, and any structural modifications needed. More complex setups, such as new chimney construction or extensive framing work, may require a follow-up visit. We'll provide a clear timeline before work begins.
Do I need a building permit for fireplace installation?
Yes. Fireplace and stove installations require permits and inspections in Franklin County and most Columbus-area jurisdictions. Blackburns handles all permit coordination and ensures your installation passes inspection. This protects your home and is essential for insurance and resale.
What clearances are required around a stove or insert?
Clearances vary by unit and fuel type—typically 12 to 36 inches from combustible materials like wood framing, drywall, or furniture. Blackburns calculates the exact clearances for your unit and makes sure the installation is safe and code-compliant.
Can you install a fireplace insert in my existing fireplace?
In most cases, yes. Inserts fit into existing masonry or pre-fab fireplaces and can dramatically improve heating efficiency. We assess your current fireplace, confirm it's structurally sound, and ensure the insert is properly sized, vented, and sealed for maximum performance.
