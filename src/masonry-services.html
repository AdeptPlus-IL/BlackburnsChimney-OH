THE PROBLEM

Damaged Masonry Doesn't Fix Itself

Chimney masonry takes a beating year after year — freeze-thaw cycles, moisture infiltration, and years of heat exposure wear down mortar joints, brick faces, and the chimney crown.

Signs your masonry needs attention:

  • ◆Crumbling or missing mortar between bricks (spalling)
  • ◆White staining (efflorescence) on the chimney exterior
  • ◆Cracked or deteriorating chimney crown
  • ◆Brick faces flaking or breaking apart
  • ◆Water stains on interior walls near the fireplace
  • ◆Visible gaps in the flashing at the roofline

The good news: most masonry damage is repairable when caught early. Blackburns' technicians assess the full extent of damage — from the crown to the base — and recommend only the work that's actually needed.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Masonry Services — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my chimney masonry needs repair?
Common signs include white staining (efflorescence) on the brick exterior, crumbling or missing mortar between bricks, visible cracks in the chimney crown, flaking or spalling brick faces, and water stains on the interior walls near your fireplace. If you're noticing any of these, a professional inspection is the right first step.
What is tuckpointing and do I need it?
Tuckpointing is the process of removing deteriorated mortar from the joints between bricks and replacing it with fresh mortar. It's one of the most common and cost-effective masonry repairs. If your mortar joints are crumbling, recessed, or cracking, tuckpointing restores the structural integrity and weather resistance of your chimney before larger problems develop.
Can you match the color of my existing brick and mortar?
Yes. We take care to match replacement materials — both brick and mortar — as closely as possible to your existing chimney. While a perfect match isn't always achievable due to weathering and age, our goal is a repair that blends naturally with the surrounding masonry.
How long does a masonry repair take?
Most standard repairs — tuckpointing, crown repair, or brick replacement — are completed in a single visit. More extensive work, such as a full crown rebuild or large areas of brick replacement, may require a follow-up visit. We'll give you a clear timeline before any work begins.
Is chimney masonry repair covered by homeowners insurance?
Coverage depends on your policy and the cause of damage. Sudden damage from a storm or impact event is more likely to be covered than gradual deterioration from age or deferred maintenance. We're happy to document damage thoroughly with photos and written reports to support an insurance claim.
