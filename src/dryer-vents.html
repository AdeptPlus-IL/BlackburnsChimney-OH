THE PROBLEM

Lint Buildup Is a Hidden Fire Hazard

Every time you dry clothes, lint escapes the lint trap and accumulates inside your dryer vent. Over months and years, this buildup restricts airflow, forcing your dryer to work harder, run longer, and generate excessive heat.

Signs your dryer vent needs cleaning:

  • ◆Clothes take longer than usual to dry (even on high heat)
  • ◆Dryer shuts off before the cycle completes
  • ◆Dryer feels excessively hot to the touch
  • ◆Burning smell coming from the dryer or vent
  • ◆Lint around the vent opening or on the exterior wall
  • ◆Humidity and condensation building up in the laundry room

The good news: dryer vent cleaning is quick, affordable, and dramatically reduces fire risk while improving dryer performance. Blackburns technicians thoroughly clear the vent from the dryer to the exterior termination, removing all accumulated lint and debris.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Dryer Vent Cleaning — Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my dryer vent cleaned?
The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends cleaning your dryer vent at least once per year. If you do multiple loads of laundry per week, or have a longer vent run, more frequent cleaning may be necessary. We can assess your specific situation and recommend the right schedule for your home.
How long does dryer vent cleaning take?
Most dryer vent cleaning jobs are completed in 30 to 60 minutes, depending on vent length and the amount of lint buildup. Our technicians work efficiently without disrupting your home, and we'll clean up completely when we're done.
What if my dryer vent goes through the wall or roof?
No problem. We have the specialized equipment to clean dryer vents that run horizontally through walls, vertically through the attic, or up the roof—whatever configuration your home has. We'll access and clear the entire vent run from dryer to exterior.
Can I clean my dryer vent myself?
While you can clean the lint trap after every load (which you should), the interior vent system requires professional equipment to do safely and thoroughly. A professional cleaning removes lint deep in the ductwork that a basic DIY brush can't reach, and we ensure no damage to the vent in the process.
What's the cost of dryer vent cleaning?
Pricing depends on vent length and accessibility, but most residential cleanings are very affordable—typically less than you'd expect. We'll provide a clear estimate before any work begins. Think of it as an inexpensive fire prevention and efficiency investment.
