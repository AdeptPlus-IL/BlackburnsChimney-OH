SIGNS OF CLOGGING

What Are Signs of a Clogged Dryer Vent?

Every time you dry clothes, lint escapes the lint trap and accumulates inside your dryer vent. Over months and years, this buildup restricts airflow, forcing your dryer to work harder, run longer, and generate excessive heat.

Signs your dryer vent needs cleaning:

  • ◆Clothes take longer than usual to dry (even on high heat)
  • ◆Dryer shuts off before the cycle completes
  • ◆Dryer feels excessively hot to the touch
  • ◆Burning smell coming from the dryer or vent
  • ◆Lint around the vent opening or on the exterior wall
  • ◆Humidity and condensation building up in the laundry room

The good news: dryer vent cleaning is quick, affordable, and dramatically reduces fire risk while improving dryer performance. Blackburns technicians thoroughly clear the vent from the dryer to the exterior termination, removing all accumulated lint and debris.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Dryer Vent Cleaning — Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my dryer vent cleaned?

The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends having your dryer vent professionally cleaned at least once per year. However, several factors can shorten that interval and call for more frequent cleanings:

  • Households that run 4 or more loads of laundry per week
  • Long or complex vent runs (over 10 feet, or with multiple bends/elbows)
  • Homes with pets that shed heavily
  • Dryers located far from an exterior wall (often basements or interior closets)
  • Older dryers, which tend to produce more lint

During your service, we'll evaluate your vent configuration, lint accumulation rate, and usage patterns, then recommend a cleaning schedule tailored to your household – typically every 6, 12, or 18 months.

How long does dryer vent cleaning take?

Most residential dryer vent cleanings are completed in 30 to 60 minutes, though the exact time depends on a few variables:

  • Total length of the vent run (longer runs take more time to clear)
  • Number of bends, elbows, or transitions in the ductwork
  • Severity of lint buildup (heavily clogged vents require more passes)
  • Vent termination location (roof vs. wall vs. soffit)
  • Accessibility of the dryer and exterior vent cap

Our technicians arrive with all the necessary equipment, work efficiently, protect your floors and laundry area, and leave the space cleaner than we found it. In most cases, you'll be back to doing laundry the same day.

Do some homes need more than one dryer vent cleaning per year?

Yes – while once a year is the baseline recommendation from the NFPA, certain households generate lint faster or have vent setups that trap it more easily, making semi-annual (or even quarterly) cleanings the safer choice.

Homes that often need more frequent cleanings include:

  • Large households running 5+ loads of laundry per week
  • Homes with shedding pets (dog or cat hair compounds lint buildup quickly)
  • Long vent runs over 10 feet, or runs with multiple elbows and bends
  • Dryers located in basements, interior closets, or far from an exterior wall
  • Rooftop vent terminations, which are more prone to bird nests and debris
  • Older dryers that produce more lint than newer high-efficiency models
  • Households where someone has asthma, allergies, or respiratory sensitivities
  • Homes that have skipped cleanings for 2+ years (heavy buildup re-establishes faster)

During your first cleaning, our technician will assess your vent length, configuration, lint accumulation rate, and household usage, then recommend a personalized schedule – typically every 6 months for high-use homes and every 12 months for average households.

Can I clean my dryer vent myself?

Cleaning the lint trap after every load is something every homeowner should do – but the interior duct system is a different story. Here's why most homeowners are better off hiring a professional:

  • Consumer-grade brushes typically can't reach deep enough into long or angled vents
  • DIY cleaning often pushes lint further into the ductwork instead of removing it
  • Disconnecting and reconnecting the vent improperly can create gas, fire, or moisture risks
  • Roof and second-story vents require safe ladder access and the right equipment
  • Without a proper inspection, you won't catch crushed ducts, animal nests, or damaged vent caps

Our technicians bring commercial rotary equipment, HEPA-grade vacuums, and a trained eye for hazards – ensuring the vent is fully cleared and safely reconnected.

Can a clogged dryer vent really cause a house fire?

Yes – and it's a serious, well-documented risk. According to FEMA and NFPA data, U.S. fire departments respond to roughly 13,000–15,000 dryer fires every year, causing dozens of deaths, hundreds of injuries, and over $200 million in property damage annually. The leading cause, by a wide margin, is failure to clean the dryer vent and the lint buildup that comes with it.

Here's how a clogged vent turns into a fire:

  • Lint is highly flammable and accumulates inside the vent over time
  • Restricted airflow forces the dryer to run hotter and longer to dry each load
  • Excess heat builds up inside the drum and ductwork with nowhere to escape
  • A spark, an overheated heating element, or a hot lint particle can ignite the trapped lint
  • Fire travels quickly through the duct and can spread into walls, attics, or the laundry room before smoke alarms react

Warning signs that your fire risk is already elevated include:

  • Clothes taking much longer than usual to dry
  • The dryer or laundry room feeling unusually hot to the touch
  • A burning or scorched smell during or after a cycle
  • Visible lint around the exterior vent opening or behind the dryer
  • The dryer shutting itself off mid-cycle due to overheating

The good news: an annual professional cleaning is the single most effective way to eliminate this risk.
