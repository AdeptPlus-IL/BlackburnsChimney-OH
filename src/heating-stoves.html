COMMON QUESTIONS

Heating Stoves — Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between wood-burning and gas stoves?
Wood-burning stoves offer traditional ambiance and work during power outages; they require wood storage and regular cleaning. Gas stoves provide instant, convenient heat with less maintenance and no ash cleanup. Both can be highly efficient when properly installed. We can help you decide based on your home, lifestyle, and heating goals.
Do I need a chimney for a heating stove?
Most heating stoves require proper venting—either through an existing chimney or a new vent system installed to code. Gas stoves sometimes offer more flexible venting options than wood-burners. We assess your home's venting possibilities during a consultation and recommend the safest, most efficient solution.
How much does a heating stove installation cost?
Costs vary widely based on the stove model, your home's existing chimney or venting, and any modifications needed for safe installation. We provide transparent quotes after assessing your specific situation. We work with a range of quality stoves at different price points.
Are heating stoves safe?
Yes—when installed and maintained properly. EPA-certified stoves and professional installation with correct clearances, venting, and annual inspections ensure safe operation. We follow all local building codes and CSIA standards.
How often does a heating stove need maintenance?
Wood-burning stoves should be swept annually and inspected before each heating season. Gas stoves typically need less frequent service but should still be inspected yearly. We offer maintenance plans to keep your stove running safely and efficiently.
