COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions About Fireplace Inserts & Stoves

What's the difference between a fireplace insert and a wood-burning stove?
A fireplace insert fits inside an existing fireplace opening, converting it into a more efficient heating source while maintaining the fireplace's look. A wood-burning stove is a standalone unit that can be vented through a chimney. Inserts are ideal for existing fireplaces; stoves work well in homes with available chimney space. Our team can help you determine which is best for your home.
How efficient are modern fireplace inserts and stoves?
Modern EPA-certified inserts and stoves convert 70-85% of heat into your home, compared to traditional fireplaces which lose up to 90% of heat up the chimney. This means significant savings on heating costs and better overall home comfort.
How long does a fireplace insert or stove installation take?
Most installations can be completed in one to two days, depending on any necessary chimney modifications or repairs. We'll provide a timeline during your consultation so you know exactly what to expect.
Do I need to have my chimney inspected before installation?
Absolutely. A thorough chimney inspection is essential to ensure your chimney is structurally sound, properly sized, and compatible with your new insert or stove. We use camera-assisted technology to spot any issues that could affect safety or performance.
What maintenance does a fireplace insert or stove require?
Annual professional inspections and chimney sweeps are recommended to maintain efficiency and safety. We also recommend cleaning the insert or stove glass regularly and ensuring proper air vents stay clear. Our team will provide detailed maintenance guidance when your system is installed.
