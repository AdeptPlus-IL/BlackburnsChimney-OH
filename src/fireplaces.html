COMMON QUESTIONS

Fireplace Installation — Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a fireplace insert and a wood-burning stove?
A fireplace insert fits inside an existing fireplace opening, improving the efficiency of what you already have. A wood-burning stove is a standalone unit that can be placed almost anywhere (with proper venting). Inserts are ideal for retrofits; stoves work best in new installations or homes without fireplaces. We can help you decide which fits your needs and space.
How long does fireplace installation take?
Most standard installations—insert placement, venting setup, and safety testing—are completed in a single day. More complex projects, such as a brand-new fireplace with masonry hearth prep, may require a follow-up visit. We'll give you a clear timeline and cost estimate before we start.
Do you sell fireplaces and inserts, or just install them?
We do both. Blackburns sells quality fireplaces, inserts, and heating stoves and handles the complete installation. If you already own a unit, we can install it. If you're shopping, we can help you find the right product for your home and budget.
What venting options are available?
Venting depends on your home's structure. Most wood-burning fireplaces and inserts use your existing chimney. Gas fireplaces can use direct vent (sealed) or traditional flue systems. Stoves may use existing chimneys or new venting installed during setup. Our technicians assess your home and recommend the safest, most efficient option.
Are fireplace installations covered by homeowners insurance?
Coverage varies by policy. Some insurers offer discounts for properly installed fireplaces and stoves, while others have restrictions on wood-burning units. We recommend checking with your agent. We provide detailed documentation of every installation for your records and insurance claim support.
