CHIMNEY SWEEPING

Why Regular Chimney Sweeping Matters

Your chimney works hard to safely vent smoke and gases from your fireplace or wood stove. Over time, soot, creosote, and debris accumulate inside the flue, reducing airflow and creating potential hazards. Professional chimney sweeping removes this buildup, helping your chimney perform as efficiently as possible and reducing the risk of costly damage.

Benefits of Professional Chimney Sweeping

  • ◆Reduces creosote and soot buildup that can restrict airflow
  • ◆Helps prevent dangerous chimney fires
  • ◆Improves draft and reduces smoke or odors in your home
  • ◆Identifies blockages from debris, nests, or other obstructions
  • ◆Detects early signs of wear, cracks, or damage
  • ◆Extends the life of your chimney system

OUR PROCESS

What's Included in Our Chimney Sweeping Service

Every chimney sweep we perform in Columbus follows the same proven, step-by-step process. Our CSIA-certified technicians serve homes throughout Franklin, Delaware, and Union counties, combining careful preparation with thorough cleaning and professional documentation — so you know exactly what was done and what condition your chimney is in.

1. Pre-Sweep Inspection

We start with a Level 1 visual inspection of your firebox, damper, smoke chamber, and flue to identify creosote levels, obstructions, and any concerns before cleaning begins.

2. Protective Setup in the Home

We lay down drop cloths and protective runners from the door to the hearth, and seal off the firebox opening to contain dust and soot inside your Columbus home.

3. Manual Brushing of the Flue

Using professional-grade brushes and rods sized to your specific flue, we manually scrub creosote, soot, and debris from the liner — top to bottom — for a complete clean.

4. HEPA Vacuuming & Cleanup

A HEPA-filtered vacuum runs throughout the sweep to capture fine particles, then we thoroughly clean the firebox and surrounding hearth area before we leave.

5. Camera-Assisted Inspection

After cleaning, we run a high-resolution video camera through the flue to check the liner, joints, and crown for cracks, gaps, or hidden damage you can't see from below.

6. Detailed Written Report

You receive a clear written report with photos, findings, and any recommended repairs — explained in plain language so you can make informed decisions about your chimney.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Chimney Sweeping FAQs

How often should my chimney be swept?
The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends annual inspection and sweeping if needed. If you use your fireplace or wood stove regularly, annual sweeping is especially important. We customize our recommendations based on your usage and local conditions.
What is creosote and why does it need to be removed?
Creosote is a dark, sticky substance that forms when wood smoke condenses inside your chimney. It accumulates over time and can ignite at high temperatures, creating a dangerous chimney fire. Regular sweeping removes creosote buildup before it becomes a hazard.
Will chimney sweeping make a mess in my home?
We take cleanliness seriously. Our technicians lay down protective runners from your door to the fireplace, use drop cloths to contain debris, and clean up thoroughly after the job. You can expect minimal disturbance to your home.
How long does a chimney sweeping appointment take?
Most chimney sweeping appointments take 1–2 hours, depending on your chimney's condition and any additional inspections or recommendations. We'll give you a time frame when you schedule.
Is chimney sweeping necessary if I don't use my fireplace often?
Yes. Even seasonal use can result in creosote buildup. Additionally, unused chimneys can develop blockages from debris, animal nests, or weather damage. Annual inspection and sweeping help ensure your chimney is ready and safer whenever you use it.
