SPRING CHIMNEY CARE

Why Spring Is the Perfect Time for Chimney Care

As winter winds down and warmer weather arrives, homeowners naturally start thinking about outdoor maintenance. But one area many overlook is their chimney. Spring is actually the ideal season to have your chimney inspected, cleaned, and maintained. After months of heating fires, creosote buildup, animal nests, or water damage may have accumulated inside your flue. A professional spring inspection catches these issues before they become safety hazards or costly repairs.

Why spring inspections matter:

  • ◆Winter weather takes a toll on chimneys through freeze-thaw cycles that crack masonry
  • ◆Water seeping into mortar joints can cause serious structural damage over time
  • ◆Heavy snow or ice can damage caps and flashing during the coldest months
  • ◆Creosote buildup from winter fires creates a fire hazard if left unaddressed
  • ◆Spring inspections prepare your chimney before summer storm season arrives
  • ◆CSIA-certified technicians identify problems early for convenient, non-emergency repairs

Winter weather takes a toll on chimneys. Freeze-thaw cycles can crack masonry, water can seep into mortar joints, and heavy snow or ice can damage caps and flashing. By inspecting your chimney in spring, our CSIA-certified technicians identify these problems early, allowing you to make repairs at a convenient time—not during an emergency mid-winter. Spring inspections also prepare your chimney for summer storms. High winds, heavy rain, and humidity can cause additional wear. A professional evaluation ensures your chimney is properly sealed, flashed, and protected before storm season arrives.
