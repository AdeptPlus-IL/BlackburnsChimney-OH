THE PROBLEM

A Failing Liner Puts Your Home at Risk

Your chimney's liner is the barrier between hot gases and the masonry structure of your home. Over time, exposure to heat, moisture, and acidic combustion byproducts causes liners to crack, deteriorate, or separate from the chimney walls.

Signs your liner needs attention:

  • ◆Visible cracks or gaps in the flue when inspected with a camera
  • ◆Smoke backing up into the home or lingering odors
  • ◆Water stains or moisture around the fireplace or chimney interior
  • ◆Rust or deterioration visible in the flue
  • ◆Installation of a new wood stove, insert, or heating appliance
  • ◆Chimney has never been lined or the existing liner is over 30 years old

When a liner fails, dangerous combustion gases—including carbon monoxide—can seep into your living spaces. Creosote and moisture can penetrate the surrounding masonry, causing water damage and structural deterioration. And poor draft performance makes your fireplace or stove work harder and less efficiently. The good news: a new stainless steel liner restores your chimney's functionality and safety in a single installation. Blackburns' technicians assess your chimney with a camera inspection, recommend the right liner size and material for your appliance, and install it with precision.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Chimney Relining — Frequently Asked Questions

What does a chimney liner do?
A chimney liner is a protective inner tube that safely directs smoke, gases, and heat from your fireplace or stove up and out of the chimney. It prevents dangerous combustion byproducts from seeping into your home's structure and protects the masonry from heat and corrosive gases.
What types of chimney liners are available?
The most common and recommended liner is stainless steel, which resists corrosion and lasts 15–20+ years. Aluminum liners are less expensive but suitable only for gas appliances. Clay tile liners (if your chimney has one) can crack over time. We assess your appliance type and chimney condition to recommend the best option.
How long does a chimney liner last?
A high-quality stainless steel liner typically lasts 15–20+ years, depending on usage frequency and maintenance. Regular sweeping and inspections help maximize liner life.
What are the signs that my liner needs repair or replacement?
Common signs include smoke backing into your home, lingering chimney odors, visible cracks in the flue (seen on camera inspection), rust or deterioration, water stains around the fireplace, or the need to install a new appliance. A professional chimney inspection with a camera will reveal the true condition.
Can a damaged liner cause carbon monoxide issues?
Yes. Cracks or gaps in a liner can allow carbon monoxide and other combustion gases to leak into your home. This is a serious safety concern. If our inspection shows liner damage, we recommend prompt replacement.
