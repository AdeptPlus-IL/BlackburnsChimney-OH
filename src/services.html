WHY IT MATTERS

One Neglected Chimney Can Cause Serious Problems Throughout Your Home

A chimney isn't just a decorative feature – it's a critical part of your home's ventilation and fire safety system. When any part of it fails, the consequences can range from an unpleasant smoking fireplace to carbon monoxide exposure, water damage, or a chimney fire. Regular, professional chimney service is how you stay ahead of all of it.

Prevents Chimney Fires

Annual sweeping removes creosote – the flammable byproduct of wood burning – before it can ignite inside your flue.

Blocks Carbon Monoxide Entry

A cracked flue or deteriorated liner can allow deadly combustion gases to seep into your living space undetected.

Stops Costly Water Damage

Leaking chimneys allow moisture into your walls, ceilings, and framing – damage that compounds quickly and costs far more to repair later.

Fixes Smoke & Draft Problems

Poor airflow pushes smoke back into your home. Diagnosing and correcting the cause makes your fireplace enjoyable again.

Extends Your Chimney's Life

Catching minor deterioration in mortar, liners, and caps early prevents the kind of structural damage that requires major reconstruction.

Protects Your Dryer From Fire

Clogged dryer vents are a leading cause of house fires. Professional vent cleaning is a quick service with serious safety value.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Chimney Services – Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my chimney swept and inspected?
The Chimney Safety Institute of America (CSIA) recommends an annual inspection for all chimneys, fireplaces, and venting systems. If you use your fireplace regularly, annual sweeping is also recommended to remove creosote buildup. Even if you rarely use your fireplace, an annual inspection is the best way to catch deterioration, animal intrusion, or moisture damage early.
What's the difference between a Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 chimney inspection?
A Level 1 inspection covers accessible portions of the chimney exterior and interior – the standard for a chimney that's been regularly maintained. A Level 2 inspection goes further, using video camera equipment to examine the full flue interior, and is typically required when buying or selling a home or after a chimney fire or other significant event. A Level 3 inspection involves removal of certain components to access concealed areas and is reserved for situations where serious hazards are suspected.
My fireplace smokes into the room. Can you fix that?
Yes. A smoking fireplace is one of the most common chimney complaints, and there are several possible causes – a blocked flue, incorrect chimney height, negative air pressure in the home, a damaged damper, or a flue that's the wrong size for the firebox. Our technicians diagnose the specific cause and recommend the right solution rather than guessing.
How do I know if my chimney liner needs to be replaced?
Signs that a liner may need replacement include visible cracks or gaps found during a camera inspection, flaking tile fragments at the base of the firebox, evidence of water intrusion, or a chimney that's venting a new gas appliance through an older clay tile liner. A stainless steel relining is often the most durable and cost-effective solution. Our technicians will assess your liner's condition and give you an honest recommendation.
What causes a chimney to leak, and how do you fix it?
Chimney leaks typically originate from a cracked or missing chimney cap, a deteriorated chimney crown, damaged flashing at the roofline, or spalling masonry that allows water to penetrate the brick. In some cases, it's a combination of sources. We diagnose the origin of the leak rather than just treating the symptoms, then repair or replace the specific components responsible – so the fix lasts.
