COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions About Chimney Services in New Albany

How often should I have my chimney inspected?
The Chimney Safety Institute of America (CSIA) recommends at least one inspection per year, ideally before the heating season. If you use your fireplace or stove regularly, annual inspections help catch problems early and keep your home safe.
What's the difference between a Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 chimney inspection?
A Level 1 is a basic visual inspection for chimneys in regular use with no known issues. A Level 2 includes a video camera inspection and is often required for home sales or after fires. A Level 3 involves more extensive evaluation, usually after significant damage. We can help you determine which level you need.
How much does a chimney inspection cost?
Inspection costs vary based on the complexity of your chimney and the type of inspection needed. We provide transparent pricing upfront and are happy to discuss your options when you call. Contact us at 614-344-7750 for a quote.
What is creosote and why is it dangerous?
Creosote is a flammable byproduct of burning wood that builds up inside your flue over time. If not removed regularly through chimney sweeping, it can ignite and cause a dangerous chimney fire. Regular cleaning removes this buildup and protects your home.
Do you service gas fireplaces and stoves?
Yes! We inspect, clean, and repair gas fireplaces, gas stoves, and wood-burning stoves. Each type requires different expertise, and our technicians are trained in all of them.
